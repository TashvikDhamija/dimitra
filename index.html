Dimitra: Audio-driven diffusion model for expressive talking head generation. Supplementary materials

Video examples

1. Videos associated to main paper figures

Figure 1. Example videos on the VoxCeleb dataset corresponding to Figure 3 left in the main paper. We see that Dimitra generate the most natural video.


Figure 2. Example videos on the VoxCeleb dataset corresponding to Figure 3 right in the main paper. Dimitra has the best lips synchronisation.


Figure 3. Example videos on the HDTF dataset corresponding to Figure 4 left in the main paper. We see that Dimitra is able to generate facial expression and lip motion corresponding to stress in the audio.


Figure 4. Example videos on the HDTF dataset corresponding to Figure 4 right in the main paper. We see that Sadtalker is unable to close the mouth.


2. Videos associated to figures from the appendix

Figure 5. Example videos on the vox dataset for the ablation corresponding to Figure 6 in the appendix. We test different configuration for the condition used to generate the video and conclude that combining Wav2Vec features, phonemes and text transcript gives the best results.


Figure 6. Example videos corresponding to Figure 7 in the appendix. We show that using "style sequences" to encode the facial expression as Styletalk and Dreamtalk can lead to change in identity in the generated videos. We see that for both methods the identity change and noise appear around the eyes.
